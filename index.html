Background and Problem Statement

In Kuresoi South, Nakuru County, many children who complete primary school are unable to transition to high school—not because they lack ambition or ability, but because their families simply cannot afford the costs. High school expenses—tuition fees, uniforms, books, transport, and boarding—remain a significant barrier for low-income households, especially in rural areas.

As a result, many promising students are forced to drop out, increasing their risk of early marriage, child labor, or becoming trapped in poverty. This situation is particularly critical for orphans, girls, and children from single-parent homes.

The Kanga Education Trust has been founded in honor of the late David Kanga Cheborge, a respected educator and a lifelong advocate for the power of learning. Through this initiative, we aim to carry forward his legacy by giving vulnerable children in Kuresoi South the opportunity to pursue high school education and build a better future.

Our Vision & Mission

Our Vision

A future where every child in Kuresoi South—and eventually across Nakuru County—has equal access to quality education, regardless of their financial background.

Our Mission

To remove financial and social barriers to high school education for vulnerable children in Kuresoi South by providing scholarships, support services, and mentorship through a community-rooted, transparent education trust.

Our Objectives

  1. Immediate Goal

    Support at least 50 students per year from Kuresoi South to access and complete high school.

  2. Medium-Term Goal

    Expand to serve the entire Nakuru County within five years.

  3. Equity and Inclusion

    Prioritize girls, orphans, and children from marginalized communities in the region.

  4. Legacy Building

    Honor the memory and values of the late David Kanga Cheborge through sustainable, long-term investment in education.

Target Beneficiaries

Primary Beneficiaries

Children aged 12–18 from Kuresoi South who have completed primary school but are unable to join or stay in high school due to poverty.

Priority Groups

  • Orphans and semi-orphans
  • Girls at risk of early school dropout or early marriage
  • Children from female-headed households or families in crisis

Implementation Strategy

Phase 1 (Year 1–2): Kuresoi South Focus

  • Identify, vet, and sponsor students from within Kuresoi South constituency.
  • Build community partnerships with schools, chiefs, churches, and local leaders.
  • Establish systems for application, vetting, disbursement, and monitoring.

Phase 2 (Year 3–5): Expansion to Nakuru County

  • Scale the model to reach students across Nakuru County, building on lessons learned from Phase 1.
  • Forge partnerships with county government, alumni, and education stakeholders.

Support Package Includes:

  • Full or partial tuition fees
  • Uniforms and basic supplies
  • Transport or boarding fees (where needed)
  • Ongoing mentorship and psychosocial support

Monitoring and Accountability

  • Students tracked termly through school reports and mentorship visits.
  • Annual impact and financial reports shared with donors and partners.
